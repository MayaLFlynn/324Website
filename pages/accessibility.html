Accessiblity

Accessible design: Identify a portion of the user interface that you designed.
How does your design adhere to one or more of the principles of accessible design?
How did you use validation tools (if at all) to ensure that your design was accessible?

Through testing with users, we found that remembering which buttons were clickable and what the numbers at the top of our screen meant required more cognative load than expected. Because of that I added a help button. that allows for tips, and when you hover over buttons, they give information about that button. Finally, I also added a mouse that changes to a hand when it is over clickable buttons.

In addition, I downloaded a colorblindness checker called Color Oracle. I then put our code through Color Oracle's colorblindness checker. Everything in our game has sufficent contrast, and the colors that we hoped to be differentiated are still differentiable in all of the colorblindness modes. We also tested the accessibility of our design through Canva's built in accessibility checker, and it passed all of the accessibility tests there as well. Our game in its most busy state is available to see through the different colorblindness modes here.
