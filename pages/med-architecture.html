Medium-scale Architecture

Medium-scale architecture: Choose one of the components of your project that you (co-)designed according to its architecture, e.g., front-end versus back-end.
What architectural pattern does your program employ?
What components result from this pattern in your program?
What technologies and/or libraries make-up each of the components?

I designed the drawingHandler which was also later worked on by myself and other group members. The drawingHandler takes care of every game element that needs to be drawn on screen. It calls functions to draw each of our buttons and then adds text on top of the background imagery and buttons. It also deals with checking when things should be visible to the user and hides elements in the early stages of the game before users have unlocked everything.

The program employs the model-view-controller architectural pattern. The drawingHandler falls distinctly in the view piece of the model-view-controller pattern. It presents information to the user, and responds to changes from the user. Other components that are a part of our program because of the model-view-controller pattern include the gameStateHandler which deals with the model of the game, and the perSecHandler that deals with the controller part of the game.

For the most part, the gameStateHandler, drawingHandler, and perSecHandler encapsulate the model, view, and controller respectively. However, we had to seperate out key pressed and mouse pressed controller functionality because of the specifics of how Love2D works. In addition, our testing framework falls very distinctly outside all of these categories.
