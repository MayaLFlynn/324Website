Process

What software engineering practices did your group employ to manage, distribute, and execute on their work?
In your estimation, how well did your group follow through on using these processes throughout the semester?
What, if anything, got in the way of integrating these process into your workflow?

At the beginning of the semester we decided on a system to keep ourselves accountable during each of the six sprints. On Monday of each week, we reflected on the previous week's sprint, what we had accomplished, what went well, and what we were stuck on. We also updated each other on what we had done, and the current functionality of main. Then we determined our goals for the week, and who was doing what. We added those as issues to our GitHub Project page Idle Game ToDo.

Throughout the week we worked on our individual components, and communicated through Discord if we had any issues. Then on Thursday each week we had a meeting to update each other on progress, and to discuss anything that needed the whole group's attention. When we would finish working on the issues we had been individually assigned, we would test the code through an informal runthrough or through our formal testing suite once it had been created. Then we would create a GitHub pull request. Then we would text in the Discord pull request channel, and request a review from one of the other team members. They would review and if they had no problems with it, they were merge it with main. Otherwise, they would request changes. If the changes were small, the person who submitted the original PR would change and push to main, otherwise it would go back and forth until all comments had been resolved. In this way, the main branch of our GitHub project always stayed clean.

Our group committed to this process and followed it fairly consitantly. Sometimes changes would get pushed directly to main, but those were only for cases where the changes were only a few lines of code, typically to address small bugs that came up through merge conflicts, comment changes, or small style changes. These were always discussed over discord or in person and agreed upon by the group before they were pushed directly to main.

This process worked quite well for our group for the most part. However, we noticed that PRs were not getting resolved as quickly as we would have liked, so decided on assigning people through GitHub; however, GitHub only allowed assigning one reviewer for a PR rather than the other three, so we had to go back to our method of communicating through the Discord channel.
