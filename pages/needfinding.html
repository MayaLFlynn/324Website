Needfinding

Identify relevant artifacts from the human-centered design process that address the following points.

For needfinding in our project, we interviewed people who play idle games - both those that enjoy idle games and those who do not. We also created a paper prototype and asked users what they thought of the mechanics, as well as other predefined questions we wrote. Later on in the process, we gave our game to users and watched them play, to see how they interactede with the game, what they enjoyed, and where they were getting confused.

I interviewed one of the avid idle game players, CF. This interview guided some of the graphics choices in our project - notably, the user didn't like overly complicated graphics, and so we decided against animation in aspects of our game, and also reduced the complexity of several buttons. This same opinion was reflected by another user Ian and I interviewed. We gave him the game before we added the graphics, and he was disapointed to know that the game would have graphics, because he liked the simplicity, though I wonder if that was truly his opinion or if he just got comfortable with the version he was playing and didn't want to see it changed.

The interview with CF started with me describing the 324 class and that we were making an idle game. I then asked CF questions about thier idle game expierence and what they liked and didn't like. I probed about graphics, mechanics, and story. Without prompting, CF described their prefered initial mechanics. They prefered starting simple and getting more complex from there. Once I was done with questions and CF had nothing else to say we ended the interview.

CF also noted other aspect of idle games such as "number go up", and incredibly simple starting mechanics and graphics, both of which we made sure to have in PaperQuest.

All of IdleDevs interview, experiment, and persona notes and designs are in the Idle Design repository. The interview notes from the interveiw with CF is here and has also been added below for ease of access.

CF Interview Notes

CF is an idle game player. They introduced Maya into the world of idle games. These are the notes from the interview Maya had with CF.
Enjoys idle games
Prefers idle games with fewer graphics, just numbers and buttons.
Likes ascii art or other very simple art styles
"I like it when number go up"
Likes the sense of progress and satisfaction in idle games
Enjoys feeling like progress is being made even when game is closed
Likes it when they start incredibly simple and new features get added over time.
Story information slowly revealed
Likes starting as a user and then slowly realizing you are a character in the game.
Enjoys and recommends:
A dark room
Kittens Game
CandyBox2
