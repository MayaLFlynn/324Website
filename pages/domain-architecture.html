Domain-specific Architecture

Domain-specific architecture: Identify a portion of your codebase that you worked on that exemplifies domain-specific architecture.
What aspects of your software architecture arise because of the domain your program operates in or the technology it employs?
What domain-specific/technology-specific problem does your architectural choices solve?
How does your solution solve these issues?

Idle games are unique in that they give the players exponentially better things as time goes on. This means that the numbers of the game increase exponentially as well, which becomes a problem in the software, because we must store those numbers. Lua guarentees precision on numbers up to 2^53, or around 9 quadrillion, but it is possible that users get numbers above 9 quadrillion in our game. In order to deal with this, I attempted to make a Math library with functions that would allow our numbers to remain precise past the quadrillions.

To start my project, I went through our game and found every instance of a number. I then calculated which numbers were possible to grow past 9 quadrillion. I decided that as long as the number couldn't reach 9 quadrillion in 100 years of constantly playing the game, then that number didn't need to be in my extended number class. I determined that there were 6 instances of numbers that could pass the range of 2^53. Those were the price of weapons and meeple, current paper and lifetime paper, current wood and lifetime wood, and paper sacrificed. I then went through the code again to determine what mathematical functions were used to get those numbers or used on those numbers. I discovered 11 functions that were relevant. +,-,*,/,%,==,~=,>,<,>=, and <=. I knew I would need to create a new type of number, which I decided to call BigNum, and then functions that I could safely use to create or update BigNums. This would allow precision throughout the duration of the game.

I was able to implement, test, and verify seven of the eleven functions I identified. I made attempts at two more of the functions, modulo, which I believe works, but relies on multiplication and power being implemented. As I was implementing the math function, I was finding it very difficult to find a way to store and carry the information without being about to use a modulo function. I am sure that there is a way, and that it would be possible for me to implement. However, throughout the process I became familiar with what Lua does to numbers when they pass 2^53, and what guarentees Lua has. Since we display at most 6 significant digits, we are only required to have precision within those 6 digits which Lua is able to do for big numbers without my library extension. In addition, the amount of time I would need to spend in order to get the library to work correctly and precisely would mean that our game would miss out on both additional accessiblity features and small additional gameplay features. In the effort to prioritize user expirence above mathematical correctness, our group decided to set aside the Math library, and not incorperate it into our game.
