Self-Learning

Identify a portion of your tech tutorial that you developed.
Describe the technology that the tutorial addresses and how it fits into your project.
Evaluate how useful the tool was in your work. What were its strengths and weaknesses, especially in comparison with other tools you have used.
Identify one particular sticking point to using this technology that you would want your team to know about when adopting the tool and how you resolved it.

In the tech tutorial, I developed how to access the Love2d and Lua and run your first Love2d program from VSCode for the first time, I also developed the demo. The technology addressed is Lua and Love2d, the coding language and the framework used to easily use Lua for games.

The strengths of Love2d are in its simplicity and the game frameworks they use. It is much better for our idle game use case because it doesn't center its design around sprites like Game Maker, which is useful because our game does not lend itself well to being structured in that way. It is also much better than Unity because of its simplicity to learn and use, especially in the case of the idle game. The biggest weakness we have encountered is in our ability to unit test within the language. There is no easy way to make and run tests. However, most other game engines have the same problem.

The biggest sticking points with Love2d and Lua are in the setup of the language support. There are no checks immedidately built in to the language, and extentions are needed for things like type checking and hovering over functions to get their functionality or parameters. In addition, running the program requires reseting VSCode keybindings and adding Love2d to Lua's recognised library support. All of those things can be solved without to much difficulty if one already knows which extentions and where to change settings; for example, if you cmd k cmd s (on mac) you can access VSCode keybinding setup, and then search for 'love', you can click on the keybinding on 'Run Love2D on the workspace folder' and change it to something that VSCode doesn't have preset. However, learning those things for the first time was difficult and required a lot of searching.
